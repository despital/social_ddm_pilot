Social Perception and Cognition: Debriefing

 

Thank you so much for participating in today’s study! We really appreciate you taking the time to contribute to our scientific understanding of the way that we perceive and think about people.

 

About the Study

Background: Previous research has shown that people's judgments of attractiveness are affected by social group membership; whether or not we think someone is attractive can depend on what groups we belong to, as well as what groups we think the other person belongs to. Here, we showed you pictures of different faces and asked you how attractive they were and whether you thought the person was liberal or conservatives; we also asked you some questions about your own sexual orientation. We will use this information to study how sexual orientation affects judgments of attractiveness.

Withdrawal from the Study

You may request to have your information or any part of your responses withdrawn from the study. If you would like to do so, it is important that you let us know as soon as possible. Because we work so hard to protect the privacy of experimental participants’ data, it will be virtually impossible to know which data was yours if you decide that you would like to withdraw at a later date. If you do decide to withdraw, you will still receive compensation for your time and will not be penalized or punished in any way. You don’t even have to say why, and we promise not to pry.
